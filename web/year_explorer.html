A decade of the map, year by year

Scrub or play through 2015–2025 and watch each year’s recording coverage and effort-controlled richness. Recording effort climbs steeply; the richness metric barely moves, then dips slightly in the last two years.

⚠︎ Read the dip as sampling, not nature. The recent decline in median richness reflects the lower completeness of recent uploads and shifting recordist coverage — not ecological degradation. This metric measures recording effort, not the biodiversity actually present.

Peak 10.1 (2021) → 9.3 (2025), an apparent −0.8. The y-axis is zoomed; the full decade swing is under one species — noise, driven by effort.

What you are watching. Each dot is a 0.1° cell with ≥10 recordings that year, shaded by effort-controlled species richness (Hurlbert S_rare10). As you move forward, the map fills in — that is the archive growing, chiefly across the Americas, Africa and Europe. The richness colours stay broadly the same because the metric is flat: more recordings, not more measured diversity. Treat this as a sampling-coverage time-lapse, not a biodiversity trend.
